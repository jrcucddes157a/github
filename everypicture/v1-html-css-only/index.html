Summary

The "Destroyer of Worlds". This is the title granted to the man known as Kamen Rider Decade. Yet despite resisting his fate to become this, he never once abandoned that name. He'll even at times destroy a world if it means it strategically makes sense. Something of an eccentric, enigmatic man, his past holds a dark secret, and his powers are unlimited. Despite this, he is still a hero, a Kamen Rider through and through, and continues to fight for justice.

Originally the "Great Leader of Dai-Shocker", an evil organization, he once used his powers as Decade to conquer other worlds. However, at some point, Tsukasa gained amnesia during an accident while traveling between worlds. He ended up becoming stranded in a peaceful world where he met his best friend, Natsumi Hikari, at a photography studio named after the woman. As a result, he picked up photography as a hobby, but becomes disappointed at the strange, otherworldly distortion in them, constantly feeling like an outsider.

One day, while lamenting over his inability to recall his past, an Aurora Curtain appears and separates Natsumi and Tsukasa. He encounters Wataru Kurenai, Kamen Rider Kiva, addressing him as "Decade". He tells him to protect the worlds before being reunited with Natsumi...and monsters appear?! What's this white belt device in his hands?! Wanting to keep Natsumi safe, and deciding to follow Wataru's words, he becomes Kamen Rider Decade and fights! However, Natsumi's world is destroyed in the process. Discovering it's a result of nine Worlds converging into one, Tsukasa tasks himself to travel to these nine Worlds and resolve the disturbances, assisting the Kamen Riders there. Who knows...he might just find the secrets to his past doing this.

Personality

The best way to describe Tsukasa is how Masahiro describes playing him: a "dangerous weirdo" with a lack of civility. Despite his odd attitude and self-righteous, often rude mannerisms, and even carefree, half-hearted mask...deep down he is actually a caring person, and a true hero that shines. Thus, despite the facade, he's entirely willing to help those in a bind by any means necessary. After regaining his memories and full control of his powers, Tsukasa even becomes willing to depend on other Riders for help and keeps in constant contact with them as friends. This can be seen in 2014's Kamen Rider Taisen: Heisei vs Showa Rider where he contacts the half-boiled detective half of Kamen Rider W, Shotaro Hidari, to gain assistance in finding out Shu's origins.

A notable aspect of his personality during the events of the season, Kamen Rider Decade (2009), is his clumsiness despite his talents and view on life. It becomes especially noticeable when he grows too cocky. Natsumi keeps him in line, much to his chagrin. Since then, it seems he has gotten over this aspect, but in turn has only grown more eccentric. For example, by Kamen Rider Zi-O, he seems to obsess over the difference in the color he wears being called magenta, and not pink.

Tsukasa's biggest hobby is photography and loves it. However, the constant distortion of reality that shows in his photos usually makes him feel inferior, despite brushing it off as "unimportant". As a result, he becomes surprisingly cheerful if someone compliments his photography.

Post series end, when he fully reclaims his memories, he has also grown more strategic and less brash.

Powers

Decade has many powers that align with the fact that he is an interdimensional traveler, not the least of which being opening the very portals that allow him to do so. Named "Aurora Curtains", these ripples in space time allow him to travel through the worlds. Initially, only his shut-in sister was able to open these, which a young Tsukasa exploited to explore the different worlds. However, soon this power became his own by the time he regained his memories and recognized his otherworldliness. Not only can these Curtains take him to different dimensions, but can even serve as a means of teleportation or time travel.

Despite this, he is not subject to being affected by timeline changes. This is in part due to his status as an otherworldly human, thus making him unable to be affected by this. It also allows Tsukasa to act as a "human time capsule" should a major change in the multiverse happen. If anything, whenever he enters certain worlds, he seems to gain new passive powers alongside his current ones, such as being able to understand the language of monsters like the Gurongi in Kuuga's world.

When he appeared in Kamen Rider Zi-O (2019), he gained some new powers. Including creating a Decade Ridewatch for Zi-O to use as a powerful form at the cost of half of his Rider Cards. This allowed him to continue to fight even when Another Decade showed up, though only at 50% of his power. He would also be shown in the show to be able to nullify Zi-O's titular Ridewatches.

When Tsukasa fully regained his memories after sacrificing himself to stop a world convergence event, he kept and could now control the power of altering reality at will.

The most iconic part of Kamen Rider Decade and what makes him so powerful is his RideCards. Not only do they enable him to transform into all existing protagonist Riders, but even utilize their powers. This makes him busted in terms of power scaling, as he easily has access to Riders such as Kabuto, whose Hyper Form is capable of going so fast he can time travel. He also has access to their weapons too.

Fun Facts

  • Masahiro Inoue loved his role as Tsukasa/Decade so much that he kept the Decade RideCard prop in his wallet. Sometimes he pulled it out as a small joke when asked for his identification.
  • The reason he is called Decade is because his season marked the 10th Anniversary of Kamen Rider's return to TV after a nearly 20 year long hiatus between Black RX in 1989, and the return with Kamen Rider Kuuga in 2000.
  • Decade's main theme behind his suit design and the primary gimmick to the show is barcodes and cards.
  • A hilarious detail added in Zi-O via a side-special is his insistance on the color he wears both in normal clothes and as a Rider being magenta and not pink.
  • The voice of his belt sounds is Mark Okita, an Australian born Japanese voice actor. He is most known for this role and for being the announcer of SNK's King of Fighters XV.
  • Decade's design was slightly updated for Zi-O with the introduction of the Neo-Decadriver. This added the graphic symbols of all the Heisei Phase II Riders up to Zi-O and gave him the ability to transform into them. As well, its color was changed from the white and silver design into a black and pink one.
  • Decade's camera is a special modification of a Blackbird Fly 135 Twin-lens Reflex Camera with a pink front faceplate. You can actually buy this model as the company actually produced it during the show's run! It's usually found on sites like eBay going for 350 USD in "Used" condition.
